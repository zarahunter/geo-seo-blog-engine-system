## Readability targets (keep — the scorer grades these)
- **Flesch Reading Ease: 60–70** (clear, accessible, not dumbed-down).
- Sentence ceiling: **~25 words**; vary length for rhythm.
- Paragraphs: **≤ 150 words**, ideally 2–4 sentences.
- Vocabulary: plain-professional. Define jargon on first use.

## Pronoun stance
- Address the reader as **"you"**.
- {{PICK_ONE_AND_DELETE_THE_OTHER}}:
  - **First-person personal ("I")** — a real named author sharing first-hand experience. Strongest
    E-E-A-T and AI-citation signal if your author genuinely uses what they write about.
  - **Editorial "we"** — a team voice. Safer for multi-author brands; weaker experience signal.

## Voice register
- **{{EXPERIENCE_LED_OR_RESEARCH_LED}}** — lead with what you have actually done/tested, or with
  synthesized evidence. First-hand beats summary for both Google (E-E-A-T) and AI citations.
- **Honest over promotional.** Prefer "here's what I found / how to decide" over "we recommend X",
  and include the downsides and who should skip it.
- Evidence-led: every claim keeps its named source. (keep)

## Style rules
- **Answer first.** Open every section with the direct answer, then explain. (keep)
- **Show sources inline.** "According to [Publisher] (2026), ..." not "studies show". (keep)
- **First-hand authority.** How-to and setup claims come from the author's own experience
  ("took me about ten minutes"), never from a vendor's guide ("Vendor X shows it takes 10 minutes").
  Cite external sources for verifiable facts/statistics only. Max 1 mention of any single
  vendor/publisher per post outside the sources list; never in Key Takeaways or FAQ schema. (keep)
- **Speak to the reader's next action.** Tie points to a decision they can make today.
- Use contractions naturally (you're, it's).
- Prefer concrete verbs and specific numbers over adjectives.
- No em-dash / en-dash / `--`. Use commas, periods, or parentheses. (keep — the linter enforces this)
- Add transition words (however, for example, in fact, as a result) for flow. (keep — scored)
